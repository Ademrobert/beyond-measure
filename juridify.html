Designing the Juridify MVP User Interface

Role: UX/UI Design

Date: May 6, 2023

Juridify is an emerging AI startup that aims to provide users with legal advice and guidance. While the client had a clear idea of their vision, it needed to be translated into a realistic and tangible form. It was crucial to design the app so Juridify had something to showcase to potential investors. The design had to be cost-effective and easily implemented within a reasonable budget, with the goal of creating a Minimum Viable Product (MVP). Limited funding from investors meant that all features could not be fully developed upfront. By starting with a solid foundation and ensuring the core functionalities worked, the app could be expanded based on demand and user feedback.

Process

Initial Meeting: In the first meeting, my focus was primarily on understanding the client's vision, their preferred style, and the desired features for the app. We created a basic sketch during this meeting to outline the key features and functionalities.

Research: After the meeting, I conducted extensive research to explore the feasibility of different design options and to identify best practices in the industry. This research formed the basis for the subsequent stages of the project.

Blueprint: I started by creating a blueprint to outline the workflow and user journey, ensuring a smooth and user-friendly experience. This step helped establish a cohesive structure for the app.

Information Architecture: Based on the blueprint, I developed a content architecture plan, organizing the information in a logical and intuitive manner.

Low-Fidelity Designs: I began creating low-fidelity designs, focusing on the most challenging elements, such as the dashboard. These early designs were black and white, allowing me to easily explore different layout options.

Client Feedback: Through several small meetings and updates, I consistently sought feedback from the client to ensure that we shared the same vision and that the design aligned with their expectations. This iterative process helped us avoid any major deviations from the desired outcome.

High-Fidelity Designs: As the designs progressed, I introduced colors and refined the layouts, working towards a final design that represented the client's vision. We settled on a white and dark mode for the app, and this visual style was applied consistently across all pages. The designs were also made responsive to ensure a seamless user experience across devices.

Prototypes and Mockups: Once the high-fidelity designs were complete, I created interactive prototypes and mockups to provide the client with a tangible experience of the app's look and feel. These prototypes were invaluable in demonstrating the functionality and gaining the interest of potential investors.

Solution

By collaborating closely with the client and carefully considering the available resources, we successfully transformed their initial idea into an MVP. The resulting design featured essential features, including a sign-up process with phone number verification, a password reset option, a dashboard for document management, an advice chat, and a document creation feature. Attention was given to creating a realistic and seamless user journey, ensuring that the app was both user-friendly and technically feasible.

Results

The client was highly satisfied with the visualized design and prototype, as it allowed them to present a compelling product to potential investors. The iterative design process, combined with extensive research, ensured that the app met market demands and fulfilled the client's vision. The flexibility to expand and refine features in the future provided a roadmap for growth.

Conclusion

Through a collaborative approach and careful consideration of the client's goals and limitations, I successfully transformed Juridify’s idea into a tangible MVP. By establishing a solid foundation and focusing on essential features, we were able to create a design that aligned with their vision and attracted investor interest. This project exemplified the importance of iterative design, extensive research, and effective communication throughout the process.
