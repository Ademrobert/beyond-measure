    Enhancing Content Organization for SMPL

  • UX Design
  • May 20, 2023

    • Introduction

    SMPL International, a company specializing in mineral supplements, approached me for assistance with their website revamp. My main contact person was Maarten Dennis, and the primary objective was to improve the content architecture and positioning. The existing website lacked proper organization, and the content needed restructuring to effectively convey the value and benefits of their product, SMPL 72. Through research and analysis, I aimed to create a more user-friendly and strategically positioned website.

    Research and Analysis

  • 1

    • Website analysis

    To begin, I conducted a thorough analysis of SMPL's current website. I aimed to understand the reasons behind their desire for a revamp and identify the flaws in the existing content architecture. One significant issue I discovered was the lack of effective content positioning. There was excessive and unstructured content, with key information not adequately highlighted. Additionally, repetitive use of the product name in headings wasted valuable text space. It became clear that a well-defined content strategy was essential to address these challenges.

  • 2

    • Competitor Analysis

    To gain insights into successful content strategies, I analyzed six international competitors in the same market segment. This study allowed me to identify persuasive techniques and information provision methods that resonated with the target audience. It also revealed potential areas where SMPL could improve its approach.

  • 3

    • Multilingual Considerations

    During one of our client calls, Maarten and I reviewed my analysis and collectively recognized the potential for SMPL to expand its target audience by considering the German market. After thoroughly examining the findings together, we concluded that incorporating a German-language option alongside the existing Dutch and English versions of the website would be a strategic move. This expansion emerged as a result of our collaborative brainstorming sessions, with the shared goal of serving a wider group of users and increasing SMPL's market reach.

    Content Suggestions and Strategy

    Building upon the research findings, I provided comprehensive content suggestions for the revamped website. These suggestions focused on effectively communicating the value and benefits of SMPL 72 to different user segments. By understanding the product and its target audience, I developed a content strategy that aligned with SMPL's goals.

    Content Structure

    To ensure a logical flow of information and seamless user navigation, I proposed a new content structure. This structure organized the content in a strategic manner, addressing key challenges and solutions while minimizing repetition. By restructuring the content, I aimed to create a user-friendly and engaging browsing experience.

    Wireframes and Visual Design

    With the content structure defined, I proceeded to create wireframes that visually represented the proposed website structure. These wireframes served as a canvas for the designer tasked with UI design, ensuring that all necessary information was included and easily accessible. I focused on creating an intuitive interface that aligned with the content strategy.

    Seamless Transitions:

    I focused on transferring my work to the next designer, keeping in mind a seamless transition to the designer. To achieve this, I established global typography styles and components that would provide consistency throughout the website. Additionally, I created a convenient tool with a drag-and-drop system, enabling easy construction of well-designed layouts. By doing so, I aimed to empower the next designer and contribute to a seamless user experience.

    Results

    The revamped website successfully addressed the content positioning challenges faced by SMPL. The new content structure allowed for a more organized and engaging browsing experience. The strategic content positioning effectively conveyed the value and benefits of SMPL 72 to different user segments. The inclusion of a German-language option expanded the target audience, enabling SMPL to reach a wider customer base. The implementation of wireframes and the visual design phase ensured a visually appealing and intuitive user interface.

    Conclusion

    Through thorough research, analysis, and collaboration, the revamped website now presents a comprehensive and engaging user experience. The improved content architecture and strategic positioning allow visitors to navigate the site easily, effectively understand the benefits of SMPL 72, and make informed decisions. By successfully integrating multilingual options, SMPL's website now delivers a cohesive and user-friendly experience for Dutch, English and German-speaking audiences.
