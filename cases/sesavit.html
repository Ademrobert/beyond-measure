    Improving repeat purchases for Sesavit

  • UX/UI Design
  • September 4, 2022

    • Introduction

    Sesavit is a company that offers Ultra Glow Collagen+, a product that fights signs of aging. However, the company was struggling with customers not making repeat purchases. Customers were buying the product only once and not coming back for more. I was hired to look into this issue and find a solution.

    Challenge

    The biggest challenge was the lack of structured and easily scannable information on Sesavit's website. Additionally, customers were not repurchasing the product even though two bottles were needed to see results. The product contains a substance that improves skin elasticity and hair strength, and reduces wrinkles, which can take up to six weeks to see noticeable results.

    Process

  • 1

    • Website analysis

    I started the project by analysing Sesavit's website to understand the issues with the structure and content of the website.

  • 2

    • Competitive analysis

    I also analysed competitors and their websites to see how they dealt with similar issues.

  • 3

    • Information architecture

    I used the data collected from my website analysis and competitive research to create an information architecture. This involved organising the website's content in a way that was easy to navigate for the user.

  • 4

    • Low-fidelity designs

    I created a rough design based on the information architecture to see how the information would flow and how users would interact with the website.

  • 5

    • User testing

    I conducted user tests to see how well the new design and information architecture worked in practice. This involved observing users as they navigated through the website and making adjustments based on their feedback.

  • 6

    • High-fidelity designs and prototypes

    After testing, I converted the rough sketches into high-fidelity designs and prototypes. This involved making the designs look more professional and adding more details. This step was necessary to build the final version of the website.

    Solution

    The main solution was to communicate that two bottles of the product were needed to see results. I did this by making the product page easily scannable, so that the user couldn't miss any information, including the information that two bottles were needed for results. This made it clear to customers that they needed to buy two bottles to see results and helped them understand that the product was not a quick fix.

    Results

    After implementing these changes, Sesavit saw a significant increase in repeat purchases. Customers were now buying more than one bottle of the product because they understood that it takes more than one bottle to see results.

    Conclusion

    By analysing the website, competitors, and customers, I was able to improve Sesavit's website by better structuring the information and effectively communicating what the customer needs to do for a positive result. This resulted in an increase in repeat purchases and a highly satisfied customer base.
